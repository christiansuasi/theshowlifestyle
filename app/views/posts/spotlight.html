Under the Spotlight with: Seamus - "Do what you think is best..."

Seamus is a longtime friend and has always been around. He attends the University of Notre Dame where he is studying Political Science and Economics. Seamus is currently located in Washington D.C. where he writes briefs on the macroeconomic situations of countries or specific industries (manufacturing, energy, etc.) for the Export-Import Bank. Over this summer, Seamus will be staying in Washington D.C. where he will officially intern in the Office of the United States Trade Representative.

Seamus deserves to be in the spotlight for his goal-driven personality; he's doing some significant work in D.C. and will definitely make a name for himself soon in the political world.

Check out his Soundcloud where he posts monthly playlists for your enjoyment. We got an interview with him to get a dose of his lifestyle.

[Congrats Seamus, you're our first person to be featured on our website. I'm gonna ask what you do on a daily basis.]

"Haha of course, lemme know what you wanna know"

[what time do you wake up?]

"I wake up at 7:50 if I need to shave, 8:00 if I don't. Now that I'm growing out my beard I wake up at 8 every day and its nice. Those extra 10 mins really add up."

[breakfast is the most important meal, so what do you have for breakfast?]

"I pretty much always have Honey Nut Cheerios with skim milk. I ate Cheerios pretty much every morning growing up too, so it's basically habit. On weekends I cook myself two eggs over medium with cheddar cheese mixed in and put that on English muffins. I'd rather have that every day, but it's really just a question of time. I'd rather sleep the extra 10 minutes."

[so you wake up every day around 8, what's your favorite daily activity?]

"I'd say my daily check of the new music that might've popped up overnight. I love going onto Hype Machine and seeing what jumped to the most popular charts, and then checking my feed to see what my favorite [music] blogs have dug up.

I do love doing the same thing with the news, too. I just love seeing the New York Times, Washington Post, and Economist... just completely different with all new interesting stories."

[what's one thing you wish you had more time for?]

"Definitely teaching myself music theory. I made it a project over the summer and got to the point where I was memorizing the different key signatures and scales but then school came and life got too busy. I'm going to try to pick it up again this summer, hopefully with better results."

[something you're grateful for?]

"Very cliché but definitely my family, especially my Mom. Without her I wouldn't have known how to cook my own food this semester, hahahaha."

[what is one thing everyone should try to incorporate into their lifestyle?]

"I'd say try to give yourself little things to look forward to in the day. Things like the music blog sweep I do every day always help me get through work with a positive attitude. Sometimes I'm just pumped to get back to my apartment to eat some pasta I made last night or watch West Wing. If you keep setting these little rewards ahead of you, I find that they build up and at the end of the day you did like 10 things that were pretty fun.

Everybody's gotta find their own things though. It's not gonna be music, pasta and West Wing for everyone."

[anything else you'd like the share?]

"I'd say it's just really important to keep the focus on yourself in your lifestyle. Do what you think is best for you, and the people that have been there for you. The people you want as your friends will respect you and stay... And maybe if they respect you enough they'll invite you to be on their new website."
